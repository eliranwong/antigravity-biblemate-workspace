## 1. God's Initiative of Love
These references establish that God’s love is not passive, but active, demonstrating itself in history while humanity was still in rebellion.

* **Romans 5:8 (NET)**
  > But God demonstrates his own love for us, in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Paul echoes the Johannine theme that God's love is demonstrated in action—specifically, the death of Christ—and directed toward those who are unworthy ("sinners" corresponding to "the world").
* **1 John 4:9–10 (NET)**
  > By this the love of God is revealed in us: that God has sent his one and only Son into the world so that we may live through him. In this is love: not that we have loved God, but that he loved us and sent his Son to be the atoning sacrifice for our sins.
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: This is the closest parallel in the NT canon. It confirms that the sending of the Son (*monogenēs*) is the supreme revelation of God's love, aimed at giving life and acting as an atoning sacrifice (*hilasmos*).
* **Ephesians 2:4 (NET)**
  > But God, being rich in mercy, because of his great love with which he loved us,
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Highlights the motive of redemption: not human merit, but the sheer wealth of divine mercy and love.

## 2. The Sending and Sacrifice of the unique Son
These passages illuminate the severity of the sacrifice: the Father giving up His unique, beloved Son.

* **Genesis 22:12 (NET)**
  > “Do not harm the boy!” the angel said. “Do not do anything to him, for now I know that you fear God because you did not withhold your son, your only son, from me.”
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Abraham’s offering of Isaac is the prime Old Testament type of John 3:16. The phrasing in the LXX for "your only son" (*tou huiou sou tou agapētou*) connects linguistically and conceptually to God not sparing His own *monogenēs* Son.
* **Romans 8:32 (NET)**
  > Indeed, he who did not spare his own Son, but gave him up for us all – how will he not also, along with him, freely give us all things?
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Paul uses the same verb for "giving up" (*paredōken*), emphasizing the infinite value of the gift. If God gave the ultimate gift, He will not withhold lesser graces.
* **John 1:14, 18 (NET)**
  > Now the Word became flesh and took up residence among us. We saw his glory – the glory of the one and only, full of grace and truth, who came from the Father... No one has ever seen God. The only one, himself God, who is in closest fellowship with the Father, has made God known.
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Establishes the identity of the Son as the pre-existent Word (*Logos*) and *monogenēs* God, who uniquely reveals the Father.
* **Mark 12:6 (NET)**
  > He had one left, his one dear son. Finally he sent him to them, saying, ‘They will respect my son.’
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: In the Parable of the Tenants, the vineyard owner sends his beloved son (*hena eichon huion agapēton*) as a last resort, highlighting the finality and personal cost of God's redemptive sending.

## 3. The Promise of Faith and Eternal Life
These references emphasize that belief in the Son is the exclusive channel for receiving eternal life, delivering humanity from perishing.

* **John 3:15 (NET)**
  > so that everyone who believes in him may have eternal life.”
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Immediate contextual link. Jesus establishes that the purpose of the Son of Man being lifted up (like the bronze serpent in Num 21) is to grant eternal life to all who believe.
* **John 3:36 (NET)**
  > The one who believes in the Son has eternal life. The one who rejects the Son will not see life, but God’s wrath remains on him.
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: The contrast between having eternal life and experiencing God's wrath clarifies what it means to "perish."
* **John 6:40 (NET)**
  > For this is the will of my Father – for everyone who looks on the Son and believes in him to have eternal life, and I will raise him up at the last day.”
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Highlights the object of faith ("looks on the Son and believes") and the eschatological promise of physical resurrection.
* **John 10:28 (NET)**
  > I give them eternal life, and they will never perish; no one will snatch them from my hand.
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Confirms the absolute security of those who receive eternal life—they are immune to eternal destruction (*apollymi*).
* **John 11:25–26 (NET)**
  > Jesus said to her, “I am the resurrection and the life. The one who believes in me will live even if he dies, and the one who lives and believes in me will never die. Do you believe this?”
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Amplifies the quality of *zōēn aiōnion* as victorious over physical death.

## 4. The World and the Message of Reconciliation
These passages show the scope of the mission (the world) and the mechanism of reconciliation.

* **John 1:29 (NET)**
  > On the next day John saw Jesus coming toward him and said, “Look, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world!
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: The Son is given as a sacrificial Lamb to bear the sins of the *kosmos*.
* **2 Corinthians 5:19 (NET)**
  > In other words, in Christ God was reconciling the world to himself, not counting people’s trespasses against them, and he has given us the message of reconciliation.
  * *Connection to John 3:16*: Paul describes the historical outworking of John 3:16: God in Christ actively reconciling the rebellious *kosmos* to Himself through the non-imputation of sins.
