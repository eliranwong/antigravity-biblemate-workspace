# BibleMate Study: John 3:16 Devotion and Exegesis

## Original User Request
Write a devotion and exegesis on John 3:16

## Refined User Request
Perform a comprehensive passage study, exegetical analysis, and devotional reflection on John 3:16.
Exegesis must include:
1. Historical and textual context: Nicodemus' night dialogue (John 3:1-21) and the broader narrative structure.
2. Original language analysis: Word studies on key terms like "so loved" (οὕτως ἠγάπησεν), "gave" (ἔδωκεν), "only begotten" (μονογενῆ), "whoever believes" (πᾶς ὁ πιστεύων), "perish" (ἀπόληται), and "eternal life" (ζωὴν αἰώνιον), referencing grammar and Greek morphology.
3. Cross-reference mapping: Old Testament references (like Numbers 21:9 - the bronze serpent) and NT connections (1 John, Romans, Galatians).
4. Commentary synthesis: Insight from classic commentaries (Barnes, Gill, Henry, Calvin, Pulpit, Vincent) and modern summaries.
Theological synthesis must include:
1. Christology (the Son of God sent by the Father).
2. Soteriology (salvation through faith alone, grace, deliverance from perishing).
3. The character of God (divine love, grace, sovereign initiative).
Devotional reflection and application must include:
1. Practical application for the believer (repentance, faith, responding to God's love).
2. Evocative, evangelistic preaching outline/points inspired by the Gospel message (with Billy Graham tone of repentance, grace, and personal decision).
3. Pastoral comforting and scriptural prayer in the first person.
