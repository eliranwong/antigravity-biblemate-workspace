# Step 006: Greek Lexicon Definitions (John 3:16)

**Persona**: Oxford Bible Scholar  
**Reference**: John 3:16  
**Lexicons Synthesized**: Thayer's Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament (THAYER), Bauer-Danker-Arndt-Gingrich Greek-English Lexicon of the New Testament (BDAGX)

---

## 1. οὕτως (*Houtōs*) — Strong's G3779
* **Lexical Entry**: οὕτως (adverb of manner)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * In this manner, thus, so. It refers to what precedes or what follows.
  * In John 3:16, it indicates the *intensity* and *manner* of God's love: "in this manner... that He gave."
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * Adverb of manner; "thus, so, in this way, in this manner."
  * Marks a high degree of intensity in some contexts, but primarily denotes the *way* or *manner* in which an action is performed. In John 3:16: "For this is the way God loved the world: He gave..."

---

## 2. ἀγαπάω (*Agapaō*) — Strong's G25
* **Lexical Entry**: ἀγαπάω (verb)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * To love, to be full of goodwill, to exhibit benevolent concern.
  * Unlike *phileō* (which denotes personal affection, fondness, or emotional attachment), *agapaō* denotes a deliberate, volitional, and benevolent love that seeks the highest good of the other, often expressed in self-sacrifice.
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * "To have warm regard for, to love, cherish."
  * Used of God's love for humanity. It describes a deep, active interest in the well-being of the beloved. The aorist indicative *ēgapēsen* in John 3:16 points to a specific, historical manifestation of this love in the sending of Christ.

---

## 3. κόσμος (*Kosmos*) — Strong's G2889
* **Lexical Entry**: κόσμος (noun, masculine)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. An apt and harmonious arrangement or constitution, order.
  * 2. The universe, the world.
  * 3. The inhabitants of the earth, men, the human race.
  * 4. In a moral sense, the mass of men alienated from God and hostile to the cause of Christ.
  * In John 3:16: The whole human race, alienated from God and exposed to ruin, yet the object of His benevolence.
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. Adornment, ordering.
  * 2. The world as the sum total of creation.
  * 3. Humanity in general: "the inhabitants of the earth, the world."
  * 4. The world as alienated from God, in opposition to His will.
  * John 3:16 represents God's love reaching out to the *kosmos*—humanity in its brokenness and rebellion.

---

## 4. μονογενής (*Monogenēs*) — Strong's G3439
* **Lexical Entry**: μονογενής, -ές (adjective)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * Single of its kind, only, unique. Used of only sons or daughters in relation to their parents (Luke 7:12, Heb 11:17).
  * Used of Christ to denote His unique relationship to God as Son. John uses it not because the *Logos* was eternally generated (as in post-Nicene orthodoxy), but because by the incarnation He is of nature and essentially the Son of God in a unique sense, distinct from adoption.
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. Pertaining to being the only one of its kind within a specific relationship: "one and only, only."
  * 2. Pertaining to being the only one of its kind or class, unique in kind.
  * In Johannine literature, *monogenēs* is a unique title for Jesus, emphasizing His unique status as the Father’s unique representative and Son, rather than "begottenness" in a generative sense. It points to His absolute uniqueness and preeminence.

---

## 5. δίδωμι (*Didōmi*) — Strong's G1325
* **Lexical Entry**: δίδωμι (verb)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * To give, to bestow of one's own accord, to grant.
  * In John 3:16, *edōken* (gave) refers to God delivering His Son up to death as a sacrifice for human sin (similar to Gal 1:4, Rom 8:32).
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * "To give, grant, bestow, deliver up."
  * Points to the active, sacrificial character of God's love. The giving of the Son is the supreme act of divine generosity, where the Father surrenders what is dearest to Him for the salvation of the lost.

---

## 6. πιστεύω (*Pisteuō*) — Strong's G4100
* **Lexical Entry**: πιστεύω (verb)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * To think to be true, to believe, to place confidence in, to trust.
  * Governing *eis* (into/in) with the accusative of person: to trust in, commit oneself to, yield oneself to Christ in faith. It implies an ongoing, personal reliance on Him.
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. To consider something to be true, believe.
  * 2. To entrust oneself to, to have faith/trust in.
  * The present active participle *ho pisteuōn* in John 3:16 designates "the one who keeps on believing." It is a continuous, active trust in Christ, not a one-time mental assent.

---

## 7. ἀπόλλυμι (*Apollymi*) — Strong's G622
* **Lexical Entry**: ἀπόλλυμι (verb)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * Active: To destroy, to ruin, to put to death.
  * Middle/Passive: To perish, to be lost, to go to ruin.
  * In John 3:16, the aorist middle subjunctive *apolētai* refers to the loss of eternal salvation, being cast into hell, or experiencing eternal death/ruin.
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. Active: To cause or experience destruction, ruin, destroy.
  * 2. Middle/Passive: To perish, be ruined, lose life.
  * In John 3:16, it is contrasted with *zōēn aiōnion* (eternal life), denoting the ultimate destiny of those who remain separated from Christ—eternal ruin, destruction, and exclusion from God's presence.

---

## 8. αἰώνιος (*Aiōnios*) — Strong's G166
* **Lexical Entry**: αἰώνιος, -ον (adjective)
* **Thayer Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. Without beginning or end, eternal (of God).
  * 2. Without end, never-ending, everlasting.
  * In the phrase *zōē aiōnios*, it describes the life that is without end, but also the divine life which belongs to the kingdom of God and the future age.
* **BDAGX Definitive Focus**:
  * 1. Pertaining to a long period of time, long ago.
  * 2. Pertaining to a period of time without beginning or end, eternal (of God).
  * 3. Pertaining to a period of unending duration, without end, everlasting.
  * In John 3:16, *zōē aiōnios* designates the life in the Reign of God—a quality of life that is spiritually alive now and physically immortal in the age to come.
