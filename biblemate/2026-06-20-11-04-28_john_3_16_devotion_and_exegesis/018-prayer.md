# Step 018: Scriptural Prayer (John 3:16)

**Persona**: Compassionate Pastor  
**Reference Passage**: John 3:16  
**Structure**: Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, and Supplication (ACTS)  
**Tone**: Warm, Pastoral, and Reflective (First-Person)

---

## 1. Adoration (Praising God's Character)
> *O Sovereign and Loving Father, we lift our hearts to You in adoration. We praise You as the God of infinite grace, whose love is the originating source of all our hope. We stand in awe of Your character—that You did not look upon our fallen world with indifference or anger, but with a profound and active benevolence. We praise You for Your unspeakable generosity, that You did not spare what was most precious to You, but gave Your unique, co-eternal Son, Jesus Christ, for the rescue of a rebellious race. You are holy in Your justice, yet abundant in Your mercy, and we glorify Your name for resolving to redeem us at such a supreme cost.*

---

## 2. Confession (Repenting of Our Brokenness)
> *Merciful Lord, we come to You confessing the darkness of our own hearts. Like Nicodemus, we admit that we have often lived in the shadows of fear, seeking the safety of secret belief rather than walking openly in the light of Your truth. We confess our tendencies toward self-righteousness, foolishly trying to earn Your favor through our own works and moral performance rather than resting in Your grace. We repent of the times we have loved the darkness of our own self-will, hiding our compromises from You and others. Forgive our doubts, our lack of faith, and our failure to trust Your promises when the storms of life surround us. Cleanse us, O Lord, and wash away our iniquities.*

---

## 3. Thanksgiving (Gratitude for His Grace)
> *Dearest Savior, our souls overflow with thanksgiving for the cross. We thank You, Lord Jesus, that you were willing to be lifted up on the wooden pole of Calvary, bearing the judgment and curse of our sins so that we might be healed from the poison of death. We thank You that because of Your sacrifice, the verdict of condemnation has been lifted from our lives, and we are now declared not judged in Your sight. We thank You for the New Covenant gift of eternal life—a life of communion with You that starts in the present and will carry us into Your glorious presence forever. Thank You for Your promise that whosoever believes in You will never perish.*

---

## 4. Supplication (Petitions for Transformation)
> *Gracious Holy Spirit, we ask that You would work a deep transformation in our lives today. Strengthen our faith, that we may hold fast to Christ with a persistent, unwavering trust. Empower us by Your grace to step fully out of the shadows, walking openly as children of the light, so that our deeds may show Your work in us. We pray for our families, our church, and our neighbors who are still wandering in the darkness of unbelief. Open their eyes, Lord, that they may see the light of the gospel, look to the lifted-up Savior, and be saved from perishing. Guide us by Your comfort, fill us with Your hope, and use us as ambassadors of Your reconciling love in this broken world. In the precious and holy name of Jesus Christ, we pray. Amen.*
