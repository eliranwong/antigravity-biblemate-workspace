# Step 019: Small Group Discussion Questions (John 3:16)

**Persona**: Compassionate Pastor  
**Reference Passage**: John 3:16  
**Purpose**: Small Group or Personal Devotional Reflection

---

## Tier 1: Observation Questions (What does the text say?)
1. Read John 3:14-18 in at least two translations (e.g., KJV, ESV, or BSB). In John 3:16, what is the sequence of actions? Who initiates, what is given, and what are the two possible outcomes for humanity?
2. Look closely at the comparison Jesus makes in John 3:14. What historical event does He recall from Israel's wilderness wanderings? (See Numbers 21:4-9). What are the similarities between the bronze serpent and the "lifting up" of the Son of Man?
3. Grammatically, we noted that the word "believes" (*pisteuōn*) in Greek is a present participle, meaning ongoing or continuous action. How does this grammar help clarify what saving faith is compared to a one-time mental agreement?

---

## Tier 2: Interpretation Questions (What does the text mean?)
1. In John’s Gospel, the "world" (*kosmos*) often refers to humanity in rebellion against God. How does this definition of the world change or deepen your understanding of the phrase "God so loved the world"? 
2. Why is the title *monogenēs* ("unique" or "one and only") so important in this passage? How does this title distinguish Jesus’ relationship to the Father from our relationship to God as adopted children (John 1:12)?
3. Look at John 3:17-18. If the Son did not come to condemn the world, why is the one who does not believe described as "condemned already"? What does this teach us about the default spiritual condition of all humanity apart from Christ?
4. In John 3:19-20, Jesus states that men loved darkness rather than light because their deeds were evil. What does this teach us about the root cause of unbelief? Is it primarily an intellectual problem or a moral/heart problem?

---

## Tier 3: Application Questions (How does this apply to our lives?)
1. Jesus uses the image of stepping into the light (John 3:21) vs. hiding in the dark (John 3:20). Are there areas in your life right now that you are keeping in the "shadows" out of fear, shame, or pride? What would it look like for you to bring those areas into the light of Christ's grace this week?
2. If God's love is characterized by costly, self-giving sacrifice (*ēgapēsen*), how should this pattern shape the way you love the people in your life—especially those who are difficult to love or who have hurt you?
3. How does the assurance of John 3:16 ("shall not perish but have eternal life") protect you from the fear of death, guilt, or condemnation in your daily life? How can you remind yourself of this promise when you face seasons of doubt?
4. With whom in your sphere of influence (family, co-workers, friends) can you share the hope of John 3:16 this week? How can this group pray for you to have the courage to share the light of Christ with them?
