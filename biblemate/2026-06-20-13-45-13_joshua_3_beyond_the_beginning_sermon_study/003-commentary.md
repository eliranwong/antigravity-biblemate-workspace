# Critical Commentary Insights: Joshua 3

This document compiles exegetical insights from four major commentators: **Dr. Thomas Constable** (historical-grammatical), **David Guzik** (pastoral-expository), **C.F. Keil & Delitzsch (KD)** (classical historical-philological), and **Matthew Henry** (devotional-practical).

---

## 1. Preparation at the Boundary (Joshua 3:1-6)

### A. The Three-Day Wait (vv. 1-2)
* **Constable's Chronology**: Constable notes a chronological overlap in Joshua 1-3. The "three days" in 3:2 began on the day of arrival at the Jordan's edge (day four, following the return of the spies). The officers went through the camp on day six, with the actual crossing taking place on day seven.
* **Guzik's Exegesis**: Guzik emphasizes that "duty often calls us to take one step without knowing how we shall take the next." The transition from Shittim (associated with moral failure in Numbers 25:1) to the Jordan represents a shift from past failure to present faith.
* **Matthew Henry**: Henry highlights that they "lodged there before they passed over" to examine the river, feel their human helplessness, and look up to God. It was a time of solemn expectation.

### B. The Ark as the Vanguard (vv. 3-4)
* **Keil & Delitzsch**: KD notes the shift in leadership symbol. In the wilderness, Israel followed the pillar of cloud and fire. Here, they follow the Ark of the Covenant. The 2,000-cubit (about 1,000 yards) distance was not only to maintain reverence for the holy vessel but also to ensure that the massive column of Israel (numbering over 2 million) could see the Ark as it carved a path through the water. It showed that God alone was making the way.
* **Guzik**: Guzik remarks that the Ark cleared the way. This was a spiritual battle, not an engineering problem. Jesus is the ultimate Ark (Immanuel - God with us) who goes ahead of us.

### C. Consecration before Wonders (v. 5)
* **Constable**: The call to consecrate (*hitqaddashu*) meant turning their hearts to God, setting aside physical defilement, and preparing their minds for a divine display. God had promised to do wonders (*nifla'ot*), recalling the Red Sea exegesis.
* **Matthew Henry**: Henry writes: "If God will do wonders for us, we must do duty for Him. To consecrate ourselves is to cast away all love of sin and to dedicate ourselves entirely to God's service."

## 2. Divine Instructions & Exaltation (Joshua 3:7-13)

### A. The Exaltation of Joshua (vv. 7-8)
* **Guzik**: As Joshua takes a step of faith, God encourages him. God exalts Joshua to confirm his leadership as Moses' successor, validating that Yahweh's presence is with him.
* **KD**: The command for the priests to "stand still in the Jordan" (*bayyarden taʿamodu*) was a test of supreme priestly obedience. They had to carry the heavy Ark right to the brink of a raging torrent and stop.

### B. The Living God in the Midst (vv. 9-11)
* **Constable**: Joshua uses the title "Lord of all the earth" (*Adon kol-ha'arets*) for Yahweh. This is a polemic against Canaanite nature deities (like Baal). Baal was supposedly the god of the rain and fertile rivers, yet Yahweh proves Himself Lord of all the earth by drying up the Jordan at its flood stage.
* **Guzik**: The victory at Jordan is a pledge of future victories. Knowing that the "living God is among you" (*El chai beqirbekem*) is the guarantee that the seven nations of Canaan will be dispossessed.

## 3. The Miracle of the Crossing (Joshua 3:14-17)

### A. The Flood-Stage Jordan (v. 15)
* **Constable**: The Jordan flooded its banks during the spring harvest (April/May) due to melting snows from Mount Hermon and seasonal rains. The river would widen to a mile, with swift, deep currents.
* **Guzik**: "We usually want the river to be dry before we even make a step; but God was truly calling Israel to step out in faith." The priests' feet had to dip (*nitbelu*) in the edge of the water while it was still overflowing.

### B. The Stoppage at Adam (v. 16)
* **KD**: The waters did not pile up immediately in front of them, but "very far away, at Adam, the city that is beside Zarethan." Adam is identified as *Tell ed-Damiyeh*, about 15-20 miles upstream (north of the crossing site). The blockage occurred there, allowing the entire riverbed south of it to dry up and flow into the Dead Sea, creating a massive, miles-wide crossing zone for Israel's rapid passage.
* **Guzik**: Whether God used a natural landslide or earthquake at Adam is secondary; the timing was perfectly orchestrated by God in response to the priests' stepping into the water.

### C. Firm in the Midst (v. 17)
* **Matthew Henry**: The priests stood in the middle of the riverbed on dry ground (*becharabah*) until the entire nation finished crossing. They stood as a wall of assurance for the people, showing that the waters would not return as long as the Ark remained in the riverbed.
* **Guzik**: The priests had to stand firm under the weight of the Ark in the middle of a dry riverbed, with a wall of water suspended miles upstream. This was a position of great vulnerability and trust.
