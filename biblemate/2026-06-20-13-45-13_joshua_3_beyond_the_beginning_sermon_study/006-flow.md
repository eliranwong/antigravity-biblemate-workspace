# Structural Outline & Logical Flow: Joshua 3

This document outlines the literary structure and traces the logical progression (flow) of the author’s thought in Joshua 3.

---

## 1. Exegetical and Structural Outline

### I. The Journey to the Threshold (Joshua 3:1-2)
* **A. Departure from Shittim (v. 1a)**: Breaking camp at the site of past failure.
* **B. Lodging at the Jordan (v. 1b)**: Positioning at the physical boundary.
* **C. The Three-Day Wait (v. 2)**: Silent preparation, evaluation, and anticipation.

### II. Instructions for the Advance (Joshua 3:3-4)
* **A. The Command to Follow the Ark (v. 3)**: The Ark replaces the pillar of cloud as the vanguard.
* **B. The Principle of Separation (v. 4a)**: Keeping a 2,000-cubit gap for reverence and visibility.
* **C. The Purpose of the Guidance (v. 4b)**: "That you may know the way... for you have not passed this way before."

### III. The Mandate of Consecration (Joshua 3:5-6)
* **A. Joshua’s Charge to the People (v. 5)**: Active self-consecration (*hitqaddashu*) in expectation of Yahweh's wonders (*nifla'ot*).
* **B. The Charge to the Priests (v. 6)**: Raising the Ark and leading the march ahead of the camp.

### IV. The Divine Confirmation of Leadership (Joshua 3:7-8)
* **A. The Promise to Exalt Joshua (v. 7)**: Establishing Joshua as Moses’ true successor in the eyes of all Israel.
* **B. The Priestly Mandate at the River’s Edge (v. 8)**: Priests commanded to step to the brink and stand still in the water.

### V. The Proclamation of Faith (Joshua 3:9-13)
* **A. Gathering for the Word (v. 9)**: Joshua summons the nation to hear the words of Yahweh.
* **B. The Sign of the Living God (vv. 10-11)**: The Ark’s crossing into Jordan is the pledge of the conquest of the seven Canaanite nations.
* **C. Selection of the Twelve (v. 12)**: Appointing one representative from each tribe for the memorial.
* **D. Prophecy of the Blockage (v. 13)**: The waters will cut off and stand in a heap the moment the priests' feet touch the river.

### VI. The Miraculous Execution (Joshua 3:14-16)
* **A. The Departure of the Camp (v. 14)**: The people leave their tents; the priests lead with the Ark.
* **B. The Sovereign Timing (v. 15)**: The crossing is initiated during the spring harvest flood stage, when the river is at its most menacing.
* **C. The Supernatural Intercept at Adam (v. 16)**: The flow stops miles upstream at Adam; the downstream bed dries up; the people cross opposite Jericho.

### VII. The Firm Stand in the Midst (Joshua 3:17)
* **A. The Priestly Anchor (v. 17a)**: Priests stand firm (*hechen*) on dry ground in the middle of the riverbed.
* **B. The Completion of the Crossing (v. 17b)**: The entire nation passes over safely, leaving the wilderness behind.

---

## 2. Logical Flow & thought Progression

The author's thought progresses through three main thematic movements:

### Movement A: Anticipation and Alignment (vv. 1-6)
The thought flow begins with a transition of location (from Shittim to Jordan) and transitions immediately into structural alignment. The people must align themselves with the Ark of the Covenant, maintaining a distance of 2,000 cubits. The progression is from physical alignment to spiritual alignment (consecration). The author establishes that before God moves externally, the people must move internally.

### Movement B: Revelation and Authority (vv. 7-13)
Once the people are consecrated and aligned, the focus shifts to divine authority. God exalts Joshua, and Joshua exalts the words of Yahweh. The progression moves from the abstract promise of God's presence ("as I was with Moses...") to the concrete, testable sign ("when the soles of the feet... shall rest in the waters..."). The author shows that true authority is verified by obedience that steps into impossible situations.

### Movement C: Breakthrough and Stability (vv. 14-17)
The final movement is the climax. The author creates tension by highlighting the flooded Jordan, then instantly resolves it with the blockage at Adam. The final progression moves from dynamic breakthrough (waters cut off) to static stability. The priests standing firm (*hechen*) in the midst of the riverbed serves as a physical symbol of security. The dry ground represents the absolute path of grace that God carves out in the midst of trials.
