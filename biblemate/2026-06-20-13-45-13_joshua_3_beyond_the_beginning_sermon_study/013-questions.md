# Discussion Questions: Beyond the Beginning

These discussion questions are crafted to encourage theological reflection and practical personal application in small group settings following the sermon **"Beyond the Beginning"**.

---

### Question 1: Shifting from Self-Reliance to Presence-Alignment
* **Passage Reference**: [Joshua 3:3-4](file:///Users/admin/Preaching_2026/biblemate/2026-06-20-13-45-13_joshua_3_beyond_the_beginning_sermon_study/001-bible.md)
* **Question**: The Israelites were commanded to follow the Ark of the Covenant, maintaining a distance of 2,000 cubits (about half a mile) so that everyone could see it leading the way down the slopes.
  * In your daily decision-making, relationship choices, or vocational changes, what does keeping a "2,000-cubit distance" look like practically?
  * How do we distinguish between moving in our own strength (running ahead of the Ark) and moving in response to the manifest presence of God? Share a time when you ran ahead of God's presence, and what you learned from that experience.

### Question 2: The Reflexive Cost of Consecration
* **Passage Reference**: [Joshua 3:5](file:///Users/admin/Preaching_2026/biblemate/2026-06-20-13-45-13_joshua_3_beyond_the_beginning_sermon_study/001-bible.md)
* **Question**: Joshua commanded the people to *"consecrate yourselves (hitqaddashu), for tomorrow the LORD will do wonders (nifla'ot) among you."* We noted that *hitqaddashu* is a reflexive verb representing active self-preparation, and *nifla'ot* are miracles that only God can perform.
  * Why does God require personal sanctification and heart alignment before He releases His supernatural power?
  * What is the "wilderness baggage" or compromise (the *Shittim* habits) that you need to actively lay down at the foot of Christ's cross today in order to prepare your vessel for God’s wonders?

### Question 3: The Risk of the Dipped Foot and Unseen Blockages
* **Passage Reference**: [Joshua 3:15-16](file:///Users/admin/Preaching_2026/biblemate/2026-06-20-13-45-13_joshua_3_beyond_the_beginning_sermon_study/001-bible.md)
* **Question**: The Jordan River only stopped flowing when the priests' feet actually dipped into the water at flood stage. Furthermore, the water stopped flowing at Adam, 15 to 20 miles upstream, which was completely out of sight for those crossing opposite Jericho.
  * What is the "flooded Jordan" in your life right now (e.g., a challenging transition, a difficult conversation, an act of obedience, or a stewardship choice) where you are waiting for the ground to dry up before you step in?
  * How does the truth that God’s blockage occurred "out of sight" (upstream at Adam) encourage you to obey in the dark, even when you cannot see the immediate results of your step of faith? How can we as a small group support one another in taking these holy risks?
