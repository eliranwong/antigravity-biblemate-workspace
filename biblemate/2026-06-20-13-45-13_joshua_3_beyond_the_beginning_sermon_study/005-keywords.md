# Keyword and Lexical Study: Joshua 3

This document provides a detailed linguistic, grammatical, and theological analysis of five key Hebrew terms in Joshua 3.

---

### 1. קָדַשׁ (*Qadash* - Strong's H6942)
* **Occurrence**: Joshua 3:5 (as הִתְקַדָּ֑שׁוּ, *hitqaddashu*)
* **Translation**: "Consecrate yourselves" (ESV, NASB), "自洁" (CUVS)
* **Grammatical Analysis**: Verb, Hitpael stem, Imperative mood, 2nd person masculine plural.
* **Lexical Meaning**: The basic meaning of the root *qdš* is to be set apart, cut off, or separated from the common (profane) sphere and dedicated exclusively to the divine sphere.
* **Theological Insight**: The Hitpael stem is reflexive and intensive. Joshua does not call for a passive event but an active process: the Israelites must wash their garments, refrain from ceremonial uncleanness, and intellectually and spiritually position their hearts toward Yahweh. This shows that while the upcoming miracle is entirely sovereign, God requires his covenant partners to actively prepare their hearts to receive His wonders.

---

### 2. פָּלָא (*Pala* - Strong's H6381)
* **Occurrence**: Joshua 3:5 (as נִפְלָאֹות, *nifla'ot*)
* **Translation**: "Wonders" (ESV, NASB), "奇事" (CUVS)
* **Grammatical Analysis**: Noun (derived from the Niphal participle), feminine plural, absolute.
* **Lexical Meaning**: The root *pl'* means to be surpassing, extraordinary, difficult to comprehend, or supernatural. In the Niphal stem, it specifically denotes actions that are humanly impossible and require direct divine intervention.
* **Theological Insight**: *Nifla'ot* are Yahweh's signature miracles (cf. Psalm 86:10). This term connects the Jordan crossing back to the wonders of Egypt and the Red Sea (Exodus 3:20, 34:10). It signals to the mature believer that the same redemptive power that brought Israel *out* of bondage is now bringing them *in* to their inheritance. It is a polemic against Canaanite nature gods, demonstrating that Israel's God is the living Lord of all creation.

---

### 3. בְּרִית (*Berit* - Strong's H1285)
* **Occurrence**: Joshua 3:3, 6, 8, 11, 14, 17
* **Translation**: "Covenant" (ESV, NASB), "约" (CUVS)
* **Grammatical Analysis**: Noun, feminine singular, absolute or construct (e.g. *berit-Yahweh* "covenant of the LORD").
* **Lexical Meaning**: A solemn, binding contract, alliance, or treaty between two parties, often ratified by blood or oath.
* **Theological Insight**: In Joshua 3, the Ark of the Covenant (*aron habberit*) is mentioned 17 times (often in chapters 3 and 4). The Ark housed the tablets of the Law (the testimony of the covenant). Following the Ark meant following the covenant faithfulness of God. Israel’s crossing was not done under their own banner, but under the banner of God’s covenant promise. The Ark represents the relational, legal, and spiritual binding of Yahweh to His people.

---

### 4. עָבַר (*Abar* - Strong's H5674)
* **Occurrence**: Joshua 3:1, 11, 14, 16, 17 (and throughout chapter 4)
* **Translation**: "Pass over" / "Cross over" (ESV, NASB), "过去" / "过河" (CUVS)
* **Grammatical Analysis**: Verb, Qal stem (Perfect, Imperfect, Infinitive, and Participle forms).
* **Lexical Meaning**: To cross over a river, boundary, or barrier; to transgress (pass over) a law; to pass through.
* **Theological Insight**: The repetition of *abar* (22 times in chapters 3-4) underscores the theme of transition. The Jordan River was the physical and symbolic barrier between the wilderness of probation/judgement and the land of rest/inheritance. Crossing over (*abar*) meant stepping out of the transitional state and entering the reality of God's promise. It requires a physical act of faith that crosses a line of no return.

---

### 5. עָמַד (*Amad* - Strong's H5975)
* **Occurrence**: Joshua 3:8, 13, 17
* **Translation**: "Stand" / "Stand firm" / "Stand still" (ESV, NASB), "站住" / "站定" (CUVS)
* **Grammatical Analysis**: Verb, Qal stem (Imperfect and wayyiqtol forms).
* **Lexical Meaning**: To stand, stop, remain in place, stand firm, or be established.
* **Theological Insight**: In Joshua 3:8, the priests carrying the Ark are commanded to "stand still in the Jordan" (*bayyarden taʿamodu*). In verse 17, they "stood firm (*wayyaʿamodu*) on dry ground in the midst of the Jordan." This standing firm was the anchor of the miracle. The priests had to hold their ground under the weight of the Ark in the midst of a dry riverbed while a massive wall of water was piled up upstream. Their physical posture of standing firm represented absolute trust in the presence of God they carried. As long as they stood, the path remained dry and safe for the rest of the nation.
