# Devotional Reflection: Stepping into the Overflow

**Passage Focus**: [Joshua 3:14-17](file:///Users/admin/Preaching_2026/biblemate/2026-06-20-13-45-13_joshua_3_beyond_the_beginning_sermon_study/001-bible.md)
**Devotional Title**: The Dipped Foot: The Point of Divine Encounter

---

In our walk with God, we often reach a place of spiritual deadlock. We stand on the banks of our circumstances, looking at a river of impossibility that overflows its banks, and we say, “Lord, when You dry up this river, then I will cross. When You provide the money, then I will give. When You heal my heart, then I will forgive. When You make it safe, then I will obey.”

But the story of the Jordan crossing in Joshua 3 reveals a different divine order. 

The scriptures record that the Jordan was at flood stage, swelling over its banks in the harvest season. It was dangerous and impassable by human means. Yet, the priests carrying the Ark of the Covenant did not wait for the waters to subside. They did not wait for a bridge to be built. They walked down the slope from Shittim, carrying the weight of the Ark on their shoulders, and marched straight toward the raging current.

And the Bible says: 
> *“as soon as those bearing the ark had come as far as the Jordan, and the feet of the priests bearing the ark were dipped in the brink of the water... the waters coming down from above stood and rose up in a heap...”* (Joshua 3:15-16)

The miracle did not happen at a distance. It happened at the very point of contact. The moment the soles of the priests' feet touched the surface of the water—the moment they took a physical risk in obedience to God's word—the supernatural power of God was released.

### The Geography of God's Unseen Work

There is another detail in this passage that should comfort every struggling believer. The water did not pile up directly in front of the priests. The text says the waters stopped flowing *"very far away, at Adam, the city that is beside Zarethan."* 

Adam was fifteen to twenty miles upstream! 

This means that when the priests dipped their feet in the water at the crossing point opposite Jericho, they did not see an immediate, dramatic parting. The water upstream had stopped flowing, but the water in front of them had to drain out into the Dead Sea before the path became dry. For a period of time, they had to stand in a drying riverbed, watching the water levels drop, trusting that the blockage miles away would hold.

How often does God work this way in our lives? We take a step of obedience—we surrender an addiction, we commit to a difficult ministry, we forgive someone who hurt us—and we expect an instant, visible change in our immediate environment. But God's sovereign work of blockage is often happening "out of sight," miles upstream in the spiritual realm. Our job is not to see the dam; our job is to trust the presence of the Ark we carry and stand firm.

### The Invitation to Personal Consecration

If we want to see God’s wonders (*nifla'ot*) in our lives, we must hear the call that preceded the miracle: *“Consecrate yourselves, for tomorrow the LORD will do wonders among you”* (Joshua 3:5). 

Consecration is the surrender of our self-will at the foot of the cross. It is the active cleansing of our hearts from the pollutions of the wilderness. When we repent of our self-reliance and yield to the cleansing blood of Jesus Christ, we are positioned to step into the water.

My friend, what Jordan are you standing before today? What raging torrent of fear, past failure, or compromise is keeping you from your spiritual inheritance? 

Do not wait for the water to dry up before you obey. Do not stand as a spectator on the safety of the bank. Take the risk of faith. Consecrate your heart, look to the presence of Jesus Christ who has gone before you, and dip your foot into the water. You will find that the Lord of all the earth is faithful, and He will make a dry path through the flood.
